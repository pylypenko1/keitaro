Cookie Settings

Cookies are small pieces of data sent from a website and stored on a user's computer by the web browser while the user is browsing. The browser saves each message in a small file called a cookie. When you request another page from the server, your browser sends the cookie back to the server. Cookies were designed to be a reliable mechanism for websites to remember information or to record the user's browsing activity.

  Necessary ∨

Strictly necessary cookies – typically these are required for the website to function properly. They can help you show the user relevant information, customize their experience, and also allow implementing and maintaining security features. Usually, without these cookies, the website cannot function properly, or its functionality may be severely affected.

  Functional ∨

Functional cookies - typically, these cookies are used to enhance the functional features of your site and make it easier for users to use. These cookies remember settings selected by users (e.g., language and time zone settings). With these cookies, users can avoid changing settings every time they visit the site.

  Statistics ∨

Performance cookies - typically, these cookies indicate whether the user has visited your site before. Analytical cookies allow you to recognize and count the number of users on your site and see how these users move around your site.

  Marketing ∨

Marketing, targeting, and advertising cookies - typically, these cookies track visitors' visits to your site, the pages they visited, and the links they clicked on. This information is used to ensure that the advertising displayed on it is more relevant to the user's interests. Advertising cookies allow you to find out if the user has already seen specific advertising or advertising of a specific type.
