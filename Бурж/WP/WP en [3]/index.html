Cookie Settings

Cookies are small pieces of data sent from a website and stored on the user's computer by the web browser while browsing. The browser stores each message in a small file, called a cookie. When you request another page from the server, your browser sends the cookie back to the server. Cookies were designed as a reliable mechanism for websites to remember information or to record the user's browsing activity.

  Necessary ∨

Strictly necessary cookies - usually, these cookies are essential for the operation of the site. They can help show the user the necessary information, customize its operation, and also allow the implementation and support of security features. Typically, without these cookies, the site's operation is impossible or its functioning can be severely disrupted.

  Functional ∨

Functional cookies - usually, these cookies are used to improve the functional characteristics of your site and facilitate its use by users. These cookies remember the settings selected by users (for example, language and time zone settings). With these files, users can avoid changing settings each time they visit the site.

  Statistics ∨

Performance cookies - usually, these cookies show whether the user has visited your site before. Analytical cookies allow recognizing and counting the number of users of your site and seeing how these users move around your site.

  Marketing ∨

Marketing, targeting, and advertising cookies - usually, these cookies record visits by visitors to your site, the pages they visited, and the links they followed. This information is used to make the advertising displayed on it more relevant to the user's interests. Advertising cookies allow knowing whether the user has already seen a specific advertisement or an advertisement of a particular type.
